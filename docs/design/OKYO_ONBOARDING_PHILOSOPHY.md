# Warm Appetite — Okyo Onboarding Design Philosophy

## The Movement

**Warm Appetite** is an aesthetic rooted in the idea that food is desire before it is information. The visual language begins with color that makes the eye hungry: a coral that suggests fire and spice, a cream that suggests dough and warmth, a green that suggests freshness and abundance. Nothing here is corporate or clinical. Everything asks to be touched, tasted, opened.

## Space and Form

Space is used generously at the top and compressed at the bottom — mirroring how appetite arrives first (the food image, the bowl, the sizzle of the headline) and then resolves into action (a single bold CTA). The hierarchy is felt rather than read. Rounded rectangles at large radii soften every container. Circular food imagery anchors the emotional center of each screen. Nothing is sharp except the headline type.

## Color and Material

The palette is two-temperature: warm (coral, cream, amber, brown-charcoal) and cool-relief (green for savings, soft blue-white for the proof screen). Color always carries meaning — coral is energy and the brand; green is money saved; cream is comfort and home cooking. White cards float above tinted backgrounds. The gradient at the top of Screen 1 descends from heat to rest, mirroring the emotional arc of the screen itself. All color choices are meticulous, the product of deep calibration — no value is arbitrary, every tone is load-bearing.

## Scale and Rhythm

Scale is the primary voice. The headline type — BigShoulders-Bold at 56–72px — occupies the screen with confidence. It is not a label; it is a declaration. Supporting text is small and restrained, whispering beneath the declaration. Progress dots are minimal: small, dark, barely there. The Kiko mascot is sized to feel discovered rather than placed — peeking, waving, celebrating — never overwhelming. This rhythm of loud / quiet / loud repeats across all five screens: image (loud), subtitle (quiet), CTA (loud).

## Composition and Visual Hierarchy

Each screen is organized around a single focal object: a food bowl, a scan frame, a savings chart, three tilted cards, a coin. Everything else orbits it. The Kiko mascot always appears adjacent to the focal object — never competing, always contextualizing. Floating pill labels (price comparisons, savings stats) are positioned over the focal image to add information without requiring a separate reading zone. This is painstaking spatial orchestration — the result of countless refinements until every element sits exactly where the eye wants it.

## Text as Visual Gesture

Typography here is not layout. It is gesture. The headline lands like a title card. The subtitle barely breathes. Buttons are pill-shaped with high contrast — they read as objects, not instructions. The "eating out." line in coral on Screen 5 is a mic drop: same font, same size, different color, full stop. No text block is more than three lines. No font is used decoratively without intent. The restraint is itself the craft — the product of someone at the absolute top of their field choosing what to leave out.
